# VDH Content Bank

In-universe corporate content for the blog / LinkedIn guerrilla-marketing play.
Voice rule: deadpan, played 100% straight. The joke is never acknowledged.

## Announcements

- "Thrilled to announce our Series A led by Gravity Partners." *(flagship — post #1 candidate)*
- "We're proud to share that VDH has been named a Leader in the Gartner Magic Quadrant for Vertical Mobility Solutions, three years running."
- "Excited to announce our expansion into the European market. The ladders are the same; the rung spacing is now metric."
- Layoff announcement framed as an "offboarding acceleration initiative" — the slides finally appearing in their natural use case.

## Quarterly updates

- "Q3 update: rungs shipped grew 40% QoQ. We remain focused on sustainable, step-by-step growth."
- "Customer churn hit an all-time low this quarter — fewer users falling off than ever."

## Personal-news genre (LinkedIn-native)

- "Personal news: after five incredible years at VDH, I'm stepping down. The company has graciously provided the ramp."

## The founder voice — inverted-visionary engine

The VDH founder/CEO character: the anti-visionary. He has read the entire
visionary canon and drawn the wrong — perfectly literal — conclusion from
every entry, and it keeps working out. Dramatic irony: he's right, and the
founder-memoir genre he narrates in can't accommodate that.

- **"If I asked people what they wanted, they would have said faster ladders. So that's what we built."** *(chapter-one epigraph; author's line, 2026-06-11)*
- "People don't know what they want until you show it to them. We disagree. People know exactly what they want. It's a ladder. It has always been a ladder."
- "We skate to where the puck is. The puck is a ladder. The puck has been a ladder for forty years."
- "Move fast and break things never made sense to us. We move at a safe, OSHA-compliant speed and break nothing. Especially not the rungs."

## Satire-book shapes (brainstorm 2026-06-11)

All are *artifact parodies* — funny because the container is real; same
engine as the site itself. One coherent comedic universe.

1. **The fake founder memoir** — *Rungs: What Twenty Years of Vertical
   Thinking Taught Me About Reaching the Top*, by the in-universe CEO.
   Strongest shape: parodies the leadership-book genre (sharpens ear for
   tropes to avoid in the real consulting book), serializes free as blog
   "excerpts," assembles itself from posts. Trope targets: garage origin
   (he was in the garage because that's where the ladder was), roofer
   mentor who speaks in koans, failure-chapter-as-brag (40-ft ladders into
   the 8-ft-ceiling market), the silent Steve Jobs encounter ("He looked
   at our ladder for a long time. He never said anything. I think about
   that every day."). Business-book pun canon: *Zero to One Rung*, *The
   Lean Ladder*, *Good to Great Heights*, *The Elevator's Dilemma*
   (haunted by disruptive elevator startups), *Crossing the Chasm* (we
   sell a ramp for that — one-page chapter), *High Output Management*
   (usable as-is).
2. **The fake S-1.** Risk Factors goldmine ("Our business depends on the
   continued existence of gravity"). Bounded artifact, HN-shareable.
3. **The VDH Employee Handbook.** ToS voice turned inward. "Day 1: you
   will be issued a rung. Do not lose it." Evil in a chipper font;
   anti-Valve-handbook.
4. **The Gartner parody report.** Best effort-to-delight ratio; works as a
   real downloadable "lead magnet" PDF gag on the site. Axes: "Ability to
   Elevate" vs "Completeness of Ascension."
5. **Corporate-dysfunction epistolary novel** (Slack messages + all-hands
   transcripts; *Then We Came to the End* / *Severance* lane). Most
   literary, most pen-to-paper expensive — queue behind the memoir.
